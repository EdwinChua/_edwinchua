## Key Takeaways

**Scenario B wins** if you are disciplined enough to invest the $516/month cash savings at 5% p.a. consistently over 11 years. The compounding on $110,734 outweighs the OA loss of $80,064.

**Scenario A is the safer choice** — no investment discipline required, OA ends up slightly better than C (+$1,670), and you still save $5,484 in interest.

**Scenario C is the weakest** — pays the most interest and minimal OA surplus. Only advantage is preserving the $51k lump sum upfront.

**Important caveat:** Since the OA earns 2.5% and the mortgage rate is only 1.55% (Yr 1–3), mathematically your money works harder staying in OA than paying down the loan during the fixed period. This dynamic reverses slightly in Yr 4+ when the floating rate rises to ~2.253%.
