## 🗺️ The Map

Spain is the world’s largest wine country by vineyard area. The map shows regions across a vast, diverse landscape — from cool Atlantic coasts (Galicia, Basque Country) to hot inland plateaus (La Mancha, Extremadura) to Mediterranean coasts (Valencia, Murcia, Catalunya).

## 🎯 Key Regions to Know

### 🟥 La Rioja

**Main grape:** Tempranillo

Spain’s most famous wine region. Divided into Rioja Alta, Rioja Alavesa, and Rioja Oriental. The classification system uses aging terms:

- **Joven** — young, no oak aging
- **Crianza** — minimum 2 years aging (1 year in oak)
- **Reserva** — minimum 3 years aging (1 year in oak)
- **Gran Reserva** — minimum 5 years aging (2 years in oak)

### 🟥 Ribera del Duero

**Main grape:** Tempranillo (called “Tinto Fino” here)

Highland plateau north of Madrid. More powerful, structured and modern than Rioja. Home of Vega Sicilia, Spain’s most prestigious winery.

### 🟡 Galicia (Rías Baixas)

**Main grape:** Albariño

Cool, wet Atlantic region in northwest Spain. Makes Spain’s best white wines — aromatic, fresh, high acid. Perfect with seafood.

### 🟡 Rueda

**Main grape:** Verdejo

Castile & León region. Spain’s answer to Sauvignon Blanc — crisp, grassy, easy drinking whites. Great value.

### 🟠 Priorat

**Main grapes:** Garnacha, Carignan (Samsó)

Small, rugged region in Catalunya. Wines are incredibly concentrated, mineral, and expensive. Slate soils (“llicorella”) give a unique rocky character.

### 🟣 Cava (Catalunya)

**Main grapes:** Macabeo, Xarel-lo, Parellada

Spain’s traditional-method sparkling wine. Made like Champagne but cheaper. Earthy, appley, nutty. Look for Cava Reserva or Gran Reserva for more complexity.

### 🯤 Jerez (Sherry)

**Main grapes:** Palomino, Pedro Ximénez, Moscatel

Andalusia’s fortified wine. Hugely underrated and underpriced. Styles range from bone-dry Fino and Manzanilla to rich, sweet Pedro Ximénez.

### 🟠 Navarra

**Main grapes:** Garnacha, Tempranillo

Known for rosé (rosado) and Garnacha reds. Good value alternative to Rioja.

### 🔵 Bierzo

**Main grape:** Mencía

Cool-climate region in Castile & León. Fresh, aromatic reds with raspberry and floral notes. Underrated and affordable.

## 💡 Beginner Entry Points

- **Rioja Crianza** — the classic intro to Spanish red. Widely available, food-friendly.
- **Rías Baixas Albariño** — Spain’s best white, great with seafood
- **Rueda Verdejo** — easy, crisp, affordable everyday white
- **Cava** — excellent sparkling wine value vs Champagne
- **Fino Sherry** — the most surprising wine discovery for beginners. Serve cold like white wine.

> **Spain label tip:** Like Burgundy, aging terms (Crianza, Reserva, Gran Reserva) are more important than the producer name when starting out. A Reserva from a reliable region is usually a safe bet.
