This is a full overview of **all major French wine regions**. France is the most important wine country in the world, and most wine vocabulary, styles, and traditions trace back here.

## 🍇 Major Regions at a Glance

### 🟣 Burgundy (Bourgogne)

**Grapes:** Pinot Noir (red), Chardonnay (white)

Burgundy is considered the holy grail of wine. Wines are elegant, earthy, and often age for decades. Very expensive at the top end, but affordable entry-level Bourgogne exists. See [[🍇 Burgundy (Bourgogne) — Wine Region]] for the sub-region map.

### 💜 Champagne

**Grapes:** Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier, Chardonnay

The only region that can legally call its sparkling wine “Champagne.” Made using the traditional method where bubbles form inside the bottle. Toasty, bready, crisp.

### 🟡 Bordeaux

**Grapes:** Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc, Sémillon, Sauvignon Blanc

Home of the world’s most famous red blends. Left Bank (Cabernet-dominant) vs Right Bank (Merlot-dominant). Also produces excellent sweet whites (Sauternes).

### 🟠 Rhône Valley

**Grapes:** Grenache, Syrah, Mourvèdre, Viognier, Marsanne, Roussanne

Northern Rhône = powerful single-varietal Syrah (Côte-Rôtie, Hermitage). Southern Rhône = rich Grenache blends (Châteauneuf-du-Pape). Great value.

### 🟢 Loire Valley

**Grapes:** Sauvignon Blanc, Chenin Blanc, Cabernet Franc, Melon (Muscadet)

France’s most diverse region — stretches 1,000km along the Loire river. See [[🌊 Loire Valley — Wine Region]] for the dedicated Loire page.

### 🟤 Alsace

**Grapes:** Riesling, Gewürztraminer, Pinot Gris, Muscat Blanc, Pinot Noir

Bordering Germany, Alsace makes aromatic whites that are richer and drier than German counterparts. Great entry point for aromatic whites.

### 🔵 Provence

**Grapes:** Grenache, Cinsault, Mourvèdre, Tibouren

Famous for dry, pale rosé. The benchmark for rosé worldwide. Light, fresh, and very food-friendly.

### 🟡 Beaujolais

**Grapes:** Gamay

Often dismissed, but top Beaujolais (Crus like Morgon, Moulin-à-Vent) are serious, age-worthy reds. Light, juicy, low tannin — great for beginners.

### 🟠 Languedoc-Roussillon

**Grapes:** Grenache, Syrah, Carignan, Mourvèdre

France’s largest wine region by volume. Excellent value — lots of good everyday drinking wine here.

## 💡 Beginner Tips for French Wine

- French wines are usually **labeled by region, not grape** — e.g. “Chablis” means Chardonnay from Chablis, Burgundy
- “AOC” or “AOP” on the label = protected designation of origin (a quality signal)
- Start with: **Beaujolais** (easy red), **Alsace Riesling** (aromatic white), **Provence Rosé**, or **Mâcon** (affordable Burgundy Chardonnay)
