## 🗺️ The Map

The Loire Valley stretches roughly 1,000km from the Atlantic coast inland toward Burgundy. The map shows 4 main zones:

- **Pays Nantais** (Atlantic coast) — Muscadet country
- **Anjou-Saumur** — Chenin Blanc + Cabernet Franc
- **Touraine** — Sauvignon Blanc + Cabernet Franc + Côt (Malbec)
- **Centre-Loire** (inland) — Sauvignon Blanc (Sancerre, Pouilly-Fumé)

## 🍷 The Grapes

| Colour | Grape | Style |
| --- | --- | --- |
| White | **Sauvignon Blanc** | Crisp, grassy, citrus, gooseberry — benchmark at Sancerre |
| White | **Chenin Blanc** | Incredibly versatile — dry, off-dry, sweet, sparkling |
| White | **Muscadet (Melon)** | Ultra light, saline, seafood wine |
| Red | **Cabernet Franc** | Light-medium red, herbaceous, raspberry, earthy |
| Red | **Gamay** | Light, juicy — used in Côte Chalonnaise crossover wines |
| Sweet | **Côt (Malbec)** | Used in Touraine blends |

## 🎯 Key Appellations to Know

### Pays Nantais

- **Muscadet Sèvre et Maine** — the classic oyster wine. Look for “sur lie” on label (aged on lees for more texture). Bone dry, mineral, low alcohol.

### Anjou-Saumur

- **Savannières** — serious dry Chenin Blanc, mineral and age-worthy
- **Coteaux du Layon** — sweet Chenin Blanc, excellent dessert wine
- **Saumur-Champigny** — the best Cabernet Franc in the region, light and elegant
- **Bonnezeaux / Quarts-de-Chaume** — legendary sweet Chenin Blanc

### Touraine

- **Vouvray** — Chenin Blanc in every style: dry, off-dry, sweet, sparkling. Great starter wine.
- **Chinon** — earthy, peppery Cabernet Franc reds
- **Bourgueil / Saint-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil** — lighter, more floral Cab Franc

### Centre-Loire

- **Sancerre** — the most famous Loire wine. Sauvignon Blanc at its best: grassy, citrusy, flinty. Benchmark for the grape worldwide.
- **Pouilly-Fumé** — similar to Sancerre but slightly smoky/flintier (different from Pouilly-Fuissé in Burgundy!)
- **Menetou-Salon** — cheaper alternative to Sancerre, great value
- **Quincy / Reuilly** — even more affordable Sauvignon Blanc

---

## 💡 Beginner Entry Points

- **Muscadet sur lie** — incredible value seafood wine under $15
- **Vouvray demi-sec** — gently sweet Chenin Blanc, very food-friendly
- **Menetou-Salon** — Sancerre quality at half the price
- **Saumur-Champigny** — light, easy Cabernet Franc red

> **Sancerre vs Pouilly-Fumé confusion:** Both are Sauvignon Blanc from Centre-Loire. Sancerre is on the left bank of the river, Pouilly-Fumé on the right. Both are excellent but don’t confuse Pouilly-Fumé with Pouilly-Fuissé — that’s Chardonnay in [[🍇 Burgundy (Bourgogne) — Wine Region|Burgundy]]!
