## 🍶 Sake Sets

### Lotus Sake Set — Shingama

**Purchased from:** [HULS Gallery Singapore](https://store.huls.com.sg/collections/sake-ware/products/lotus-sake-set)

**Price:** SGD $299

**Origin:** Seto, Aichi Prefecture, Japan

**Brand:** Shingama (est. 1919)

**Material:** Porcelain

**Pieces:**

- Katakuchi (pouring vessel): ⌀68mm rim × H105mm, 320ml capacity
- Cup (ochoko): ⌀52 × H45mm, 60ml capacity

**About the piece:**

Painted using the *dami* technique — painting within outlines using a thick brush with an extremely thin tip, adjusting paint amount through brush pressure. The lotus flower illustration is done in *gosu* (cobalt blue glaze) with gold strokes at the center of the petals. Shingama is known for choosing soil, glaze, and gosu carefully to create striking blue-and-white contrast.

**Care:**

- Hand wash only — no dishwasher, microwave, oven, or open fire
- Use soft, non-abrasive scouring pad
- Wash before first use

**Best used for:** Chilled Junmai Ginjo or Junmai Daiginjo — the katakuchi (spouted bowl) is ideal for pouring sake tableside, and the small ochoko cups keep each pour fresh and cold.

## 🍶 Sake Cups (Guinomi / Ochoko)

### Forged Metal Guinomi — Kotaro Uranaka

**Purchased from:** [HULS Gallery Singapore](https://store.huls.com.sg/collections/sake-ware/)

**Price:** SGD $265

**Artist:** Kotaro Uranaka (浦中耕太郎, b. 1984, Wakayama Prefecture)

**Technique:** Tankin (鍛金) — traditional Japanese forged metalwork

**Material:** Copper and tin (soft metals), coated with liquid glass to prevent discoloration

**Edition:** 1 of 5 — limited set of 5 different pieces. Only one purchased.

**Artist Biography (from original card):**

- 1984 — Born in Wakayama
- 2010 — Completed Graduate School, Tohoku University of Art and Design
- 2011 — Entered Kanazawa Utatsuyama Kogei Kobo
- 2013 — Completed Kanazawa Utatsuyama Kogei Kobo
- 2014 — Teaching Assistant, Metal Engraving, Manufacturing Design Major, Akita Prefectural University of Art
- 2018 — Specialist, Kanazawa Utatsuyama Kogei Kobo
- 2023 — Established workshop

> ⚠️ Cross-check note: No independent public profile found online to verify these details. The card is the primary source and is treated as authoritative. Kanazawa Utatsuyama Kogei Kobo is a well-known prestigious crafts residency program in Kanazawa, which is consistent with his tankin specialization — Kanazawa is Japan's historic center of metal craft.

**About the piece:**

Kotaro Uranaka works in the tradition of *tankin*, the art of forging and shaping metal by hand. This guinomi features decorative patterns inspired by the motif of rain — a recurring theme in his work. The patterns are created using a masking technique, producing rhythmic marks that evoke falling raindrops across the surface. As light reflects off the hammered metal, the interplay of texture and shadow creates a quiet sense of movement and atmosphere.

**Design:** Dark exterior with white linear rain-motif patterns; polished silver interior. Flared lip, stable base.

**Pairing with the Lotus Set:**

The Uranaka guinomi pairs beautifully with the Lotus katakuchi as a contrast piece — pour from the porcelain katakuchi into the forged metal cup. The metal interior slightly chills the sake on contact and adds a subtle mineral quality to the drinking experience. A striking visual contrast between cobalt blue ceramic and hammered silver metal.

**Best used for:** Dry Junmai Ginjo or Junmai Daiginjo served chilled. The metal cup is also excellent for warm Junmai — the metal conducts heat evenly and warms in your hands pleasantly.

**Care Instructions (from original card):**

- Do NOT scrub with steel wool or abrasives
- Do NOT use in microwave, oven, dishwasher, or dish dryer
- Do NOT place directly over open flame — copper and tin have low melting points and may melt
- Avoid strong acidic liquids (lemon juice, vinegar) — causes discoloration
- Wash with a soft sponge and neutral detergent. Thoroughly dry with a dry cloth immediately after.
- If verdigris (green patina) appears from moisture, remove with a toothpick — it is harmless but preventable by drying thoroughly
- Surface is coated with liquid glass to prevent discoloration
