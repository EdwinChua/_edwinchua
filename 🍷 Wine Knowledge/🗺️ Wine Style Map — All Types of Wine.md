## 🔴 RED WINES

### 1. Light-Bodied Red

Low tannin, high acid, juicy and easy to drink. Serve slightly chilled. The easiest reds for beginners.

- **Pinot Noir** — cherry, cranberry, red-floral, earthy. The benchmark light red.
- **Gamay** — lighter, juicier, more floral than Pinot Noir. Labeled "Beaujolais" in France.
- Similar varieties: Schiava, Zweigelt, St. Laurent, Blaufränkisch (lighter styles)

### 2. Medium-Bodied Red

The most food-friendly reds. Not too light, not too heavy.

- **Merlot** — smooth, lower tannin than Cabernet, red plum and cherry in cool climates, baked blackberry in warm climates. Chocolatey and meaty with age. A chameleon grape — style varies a lot by region.
- **Sangiovese** — cherry, dried herbs, leather. Labeled Chianti in Tuscany.
- **Grenache / Garnacha** — ripe red fruit, spice, high alcohol. Spanish origin.
- **Zinfandel** — broad fruit from raspberry to blackberry, Asian 5-spice, sweet tobacco
- **Montepulciano, Cabernet Franc, Barbera** — also fall here

### 3. Full-Bodied Red

Bold, tannic, high alcohol. Need food (fatty proteins) to balance.

- **Cabernet Sauvignon** — the most popular wine variety in the world. Bold tannins, blackcurrant, graphite, mint, long finish. Pairs with steak.
- **Syrah / Shiraz** — meaty, spicy, black fruit, beef broth, black pepper
- **Malbec** — black fruit, coffee, chocolate, less meaty than Syrah. Argentina is the main producer.
- **Mourvèdre / Monastrell** — earthy, meaty, dark fruit
- **Petite Sirah** — even more tannic than Syrah, very full-bodied
- **Pinotage** — South Africa, smoky, intense
- **Nebbiolo** — Barolo and Barbaresco in Italy. Deceptively light in color but incredibly tannic and age-worthy.

> 💡 **Merlot vs Cabernet Sauvignon:** Both are Bordeaux grapes and often blended together. Merlot = smoother, softer, more red fruit. Cab Sauv = bolder, more tannic, more black fruit. Merlot is the most planted grape in Bordeaux; Cab Sauv is #1 globally.

## 🌸 ROSÉ WINES

Still wine made from red grapes but with minimal skin contact so it stays pink rather than turning red. Most rosé is dry, not sweet.

- **Dry Rosé** — Provence (France) is the global benchmark: pale pink, bone dry, fresh strawberry and citrus. Also: Tavel, Bandol Rosé, Pinot Noir Rosé, Sangiovese Rosé.
- **Off-Dry Rosé** — White Zinfandel, White Merlot, Rosé d'Anjou — slightly sweet, easy drinking
- Rosé color ranges from pale salmon (Pinot Noir) to deep magenta (Garnacha)

> 💡 Start with Provence Rosé. It's the most food-friendly, widely available, and the global standard for dry rosé.

## ⚪ WHITE WINES

### 4. Light-Bodied White (Crisp & Dry)

High acid, no oak, fresh and zesty. Best with seafood.

- **Sauvignon Blanc** — grapefruit, grass, gooseberry, green pepper. Sancerre (France) is the benchmark.
- **Pinot Grigio / Pinot Gris** — light, citrus, easy drinking (Italy style). Richer and more aromatic in Alsace.
- **Albariño** — peach, citrus, saline. Perfect seafood wine from Spain.
- **Muscadet** — bone dry, sea shell, lime. The oyster wine.
- **Grüner Veltliner** — citrus, white pepper, herbal. Austria.
- Also: Vinho Verde, Assyrtiko, Vermentino, Verdicchio, Soave, Pinot Blanc

### 5. Full-Bodied White (Rich & Creamy)

Often oak-aged. Rich, buttery, complex.

- **Chardonnay (oaked)** — vanilla, butter, tropical fruit, toasty. Burgundy is the benchmark. Unoaked Chardonnay is lighter and zestier (apple, citrus).
- **Viognier** — perfumed, floral, peach, apricot. Rich body.
- **White Burgundy (Meursault, Puligny-Montrachet)** — the finest expression of oaked Chardonnay
- Also: Sémillon, Marsanne, Roussanne, White Rioja (oaked Viura)

### 6. Aromatic White (Perfumed & Sometimes Sweet)

Highly aromatic grapes — can be dry, off-dry, or sweet.

- **Riesling** — extremely versatile. Bone dry to very sweet. Apple, lime, petrol with age. Germany and Alsace are key regions. Kabinett = off-dry; Spätlese = sweeter; Auslese/Ice Wine = very sweet.
- **Gewürztraminer** — lychee, rose, ginger, exotic spice. Low acid.
- **Muscat Blanc** — grapey, floral, sweet. Used in Moscato d'Asti.
- **Chenin Blanc** — Loire Valley. Ranges from dry (Savennières) to sweet (Vouvray, Sauternes-adjacent styles). Appley, honeyed, waxy.

## 🫧 SPARKLING WINES

### 7. Sparkling Wine

Made by trapping CO2 during a secondary fermentation.

**Sweetness scale (driest → sweetest):**

Brut Nature → Extra Brut → Brut → Extra Dry → Sec → Demi-Sec → Doux

- **Champagne** — Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier, Chardonnay. Toasty, bready, yeasty, crisp. Most is Non-Vintage Brut. The most technically complex wine in the world.
- **Cava** — Spanish Champagne-method. Earthy, appley, nutty. Excellent value.
- **Prosecco** — Italian, tank-method. Lighter, fruitier, less yeasty than Champagne. Easy drinking.
- **Crémant** — French sparkling wine made outside Champagne. Very good value.
- **Moscato d'Asti** — sweet, low alcohol, peach and apricot. Great dessert wine.
- **Lambrusco** — Italian red sparkling. Ranges from dry (Secco) to sweet (Dolce).
- **Sparkling Shiraz** — Australian, dry, dark red sparkling. Unusual and fun.

## 🍯 DESSERT & FORTIFIED WINES

### 8. Sweet Wine (non-fortified)

Made from very ripe or botrytised (noble rot) grapes.

- **Sauternes** — France. Botrytised Sémillon + Sauvignon Blanc. Honeyed, apricot, complex. The world's greatest sweet wine.
- **Riesling Auslese / Ice Wine** — Germany/Austria/Canada. Concentrated, sweet, very high acid.
- **Vouvray Moelleux** — sweet Chenin Blanc from Loire
- **Tokaji** — Hungary. "Aszú" style = botrytised, complex, orange marmalade notes.

### 9. Fortified Wine

Spirit (usually grape brandy) added to wine to stop fermentation or raise alcohol.

**Port (Portugal):**

- **Ruby Port** — young, fruity, affordable. Best starting point.
- **LBV (Late Bottled Vintage)** — more complex, great value
- **Tawny Port** — aged in small barrels, nutty, orange, dried fruit. Serve chilled.
- **Vintage Port** — top tier, needs decades of aging, very expensive

**Sherry (Spain — Jerez):**

- **Fino** — bone dry, pale, nutty, saline. Serve very cold like white wine. The most surprising wine for beginners.
- **Manzanilla** — like Fino but from coastal Sanlúcar, slightly saline/briny
- **Amontillado** — amber, hazelnut, between Fino and Oloroso in richness
- **Oloroso** — dry, dark, walnut, rich but no sweetness
- **Pedro Ximénez (PX)** — intensely sweet, dark raisin and molasses. Pour over ice cream.
- **Cream Sherry** — sweetened Oloroso, good intro to Sherry

**Other:**

- **Madeira** — Portuguese island wine, incredibly long-lived, styles from dry (Sercial) to sweet (Malmsey)
- **Marsala** — Sicilian fortified, often used in cooking
- **Banyuls / Maury** — French Grenache-based Port-style wines
- **Vin Santo** — Italian dried-grape wine, not technically fortified

## 🍽️ Quick Reference: What to Drink When

| Occasion | Style | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First red wine | Light-bodied red | Beaujolais Villages (Gamay) |
| Easy everyday red | Medium-bodied red | Merlot or Grenache |
| Steak dinner | Full-bodied red | Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec |
| First white wine | Light-bodied white | Pinot Grigio or Albariño |
| Oaky white lover | Full-bodied white | Oaked Chardonnay |
| Seafood | Crisp dry white | Muscadet or Sauvignon Blanc |
| Summer / outdoors | Dry Rosé | Provence Rosé |
| Celebration | Sparkling | Brut Champagne or Cava |
| Dessert | Sweet wine | Sauternes or Moscato d'Asti |
| After dinner | Fortified | Ruby Port or Tawny Port |
| Adventurous pick | Dry fortified | Fino Sherry (serve cold!) |
