## 🗺️ The Map

The Burgundy map shows the region running north-south, divided into distinct sub-regions:

- **Chablis** (top-left inset) — isolated outpost, Chardonnay only
- **Côte de Nuits** — northern half of Côte d’Or, mostly Pinot Noir
- **Côte de Beaune** — southern half of Côte d’Or, Pinot Noir + Chardonnay
- **Côte Chalonnaise** — more affordable, Pinot Noir + Chardonnay + Gamay
- **Mâconnais** — large southern zone, mostly Chardonnay, great value

## 🍷 The Grapes

| Colour | Grape | Style |
| --- | --- | --- |

| White | **Chardonnay** | Ranges from lean & mineral (Chablis) to rich & buttery (Meursault) |

## 🎯 Key Villages to Know

### Côte de Nuits (Reds)

- **Gevrey-Chambertin** — powerful, structured Pinot Noir
- **Chambolle-Musigny** — feminine, perfumed Pinot Noir
- **Vosne-Romanée** — home of Romanée-Conti, world’s most expensive wine
- **Nuits-Saint-Georges** — more affordable, earthy and robust

### Côte de Beaune (Whites)

- **Meursault** — rich, buttery, oaky Chardonnay
- **Puligny-Montrachet** — the benchmark for white Burgundy
- **Chassagne-Montrachet** — similar to Puligny, slightly rounder
- **Pommard** — powerful, tannic Pinot Noir
- **Volnay** — elegant, lighter Pinot Noir

### Mâconnais (Value)

- **Mâcon-Villages** — everyday Chardonnay, crisp and unoaked
- **Pouilly-Fuissé** — step up in richness, still good value
- **Saint-Véran** — reliable and affordable

### Chablis

- **Petit Chablis** — entry level, very crisp
- **Chablis** — the benchmark — flinty, steely, oyster-pairing classic
- **Chablis Premier Cru / Grand Cru** — more complex, age-worthy

## 🏆 The Classification System

Burgundy has a 4-tier quality pyramid (bottom to top):

1. **Bourgogne AOC** — regional, entry level, most affordable
2. **Village** — e.g. “Nuits-Saint-Georges”
3. **Premier Cru (1er Cru)** — specific vineyard, higher quality
4. **Grand Cru** — the top 33 vineyards in all of Burgundy

---

## 💡 Beginner Entry Points

- **Mâcon-Villages** or **Saint-Véran** — affordable Chardonnay intro
- **Chablis AOC** — best intro to lean, mineral Chardonnay
- **Bourgogne Pinot Noir** — cheapest entry into Burgundy reds
- **Givry** or **Mercurey** (Côte Chalonnaise) — underrated, good value Pinot Noir

> Avoid grand cru and premier cru until you know you love the style — prices jump dramatically.
